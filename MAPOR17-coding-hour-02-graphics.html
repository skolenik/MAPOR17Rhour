Kernel density estimate is a version of density representation that produces a smooth line rather than bars, like historgrams do.

> densityPlot( ~ AgeofPanelist, data=Rtest, bw="SJ", adjust=1, 
+   kernel="epanechnikov", xlab="Age")

Scatterplots

Scatterplots demonstrate the relations between two continuous variables. The relations may be monotone (i.e., one variable goes up as the other goes up or down), curvlinear with change of trends, cluster (i.e., groups of observations separated in space), etc.

Scatterplots and scatterplot matrices

Rcommander can generate scatterplots that have extended features including:

  • loess smothed curves (locally weighted scatterplot smoothing)
  • spread of the smooth curve (variance+bias)
  • least squares regression line
  • marginal boxplots

Rcommander can also generate scatterplot matrices

  • histograms, density plots or boxplots along the diagonal
> library(lattice, pos=16)
> xyplot(Hips + Waist ~ Height | Gender, outer=TRUE, layout=c( 2 * 1 , 2 ) , 
+   between=list(x=c( 0 ), y=1), type="p", pch=16, auto.key=list(border=TRUE), 
+   par.settings=simpleTheme(pch=16), scales=list(x=list(relation='same'), 
+   y=list(relation='same')), data=dataBodyMeas)

Bubble plots can be conveniently used to visualize categorical variables, as well. Bubble plots applied to two categorical variables (stored as numeric variables in R with fewer than 10 unique values each) is a way to create a more informative scatterplot where the size of the plotted point is proportional to another value. Commonly this value is the frequency of the cell formed by the cross tabulation of the two categorical variables.

Another variation on the topic of using a count variable to be added to the location on the scatterplot is the sunflower plot. In this plot, each point is depicted as a “sunflower” with the number of petals being proportional to another variable – most commonly, the joint frequency of the specific levels of the two categorical variables, considered together in a cross-tabulated contingency table.
